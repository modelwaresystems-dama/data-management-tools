Know Your Value has two halves. You fill in one; your Growth Architect runs the other. They share the same vocabulary, so a single piece of work flows cleanly from your weekly log all the way to a department-level value picture.

Who uses what The Workbook is for every individual contributor — it's your personal record of value. The Growth Architect Console is for the person who leads a department (the "Growth Architect"); they import everyone's workbooks and manage the team's value picture. Shared concepts & vocabulary

A handful of terms mean the same thing in both tools. Getting these straight makes everything else click.

Strategic themes & Data Initiatives

The company expresses what it values as six strategic themes, each broken into a small set of Data Initiatives. Work tied to one of these initiatives is what the framework calls Business Value-Add — value the business has explicitly asked for.

Data Governance & Compliance Data Quality & Master Data Advanced Analytics & AI Data Platform & Architecture Data Fluency & Culture Self-Service & Democratization

The work-mix: VA / BNVA / NVA

Every activity you log is classified into one of three categories — your own view of its value:

Value-Adding (VA)Business Non-Value-Adding (BNVA)NVA
  • VA — Value-Adding: work that directly creates value the business wants.
  • BNVA — Business Non-Value-Adding: necessary overhead (meetings, admin) that keeps things running but isn't itself the value.
  • NVA — Non-Value-Adding: rework, waste, things that could be removed.

VA-aligned — the headline metric

Definition VA-aligned = the share of your hours that are both classified VA and sit on a strategic Data Initiative. It is never higher than your VA figure, and it drops when value-adding effort isn't pointed at a company initiative. That gap is the useful signal — value you're creating that the business hasn't (yet) asked for.

This is different from the raw VA percentage of your work-mix: a task can be on a strategic initiative but classified BNVA (so it counts toward initiative coverage but not toward VA-aligned).

Roles & departments (the matrix)

People rarely sit in exactly one box. In KYV a person can be a Team member of several departments, the Growth Architect (lead) of one, and an Executive who oversees others — all at once. Because one person's work can span several of these hats, each activity you log carries a Role so it can be attributed to the right department. (See Crossover 1.)

Stakeholders

A stakeholder is whoever relies on the value of a given piece of work — the people who would notice if it stopped. Naming them per initiative is what makes feedback meaningful. (See Crossover 2.)

Part 1The KYV Workbook

Your personal, single-file record of the value you create. Open the HTML file in any browser — your entries are saved automatically in that browser, and you export an Excel file to share.

No install, no accountIt's one self-contained file. Work is saved in your own browser as you type; nothing is uploaded anywhere until you choose to export and send the Excel file.

Section by section

Every screen in the Workbook now opens with a "How to complete this screen" guide — a numbered, field-by-field walkthrough with a worked example of what a good record looks like. The jump buttons below take you straight to each screen; expand that guide at the top of the page as you fill it in.

Position

Where Am I Now

Anchor the value you deliver today, who depends on it, and the proof.

What to enter

  • Today, I am mainly valued for — The outcome you're known for — phrase it as value, not a task. e.g. Translating data governance delivery into executive-ready outcomes and evidence
  • Who relies on this value — The people or teams who depend on this value. e.g. CEO / EXCO; Risk & Compliance; Business Unit Heads; Data Office
  • Progression — Is this value growing, plateauing, or declining? e.g. Growing
  • Evidence (last 30 days) — Concrete proof from the last 30 days. e.g. Delivered a data maturity baseline used in Q2 budget planning
  • Next step (7 days) — One small step you can take in the next 7 days. e.g. Publish a 1-page Value Statement + Evidence for the EXCO sponsor

Aim for: Specific, outcome-framed and backed by recent evidence.

Spot the Win-Win

Map each activity against what the organisation really needs and what grows you next.

What to enter

  • Current Role — The role/hat you wear for this activity. e.g. CDO
  • Activity — The specific activity or workstream. e.g. Data Quality programme – critical data elements
  • RACI — Your role: Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, or Informed. e.g. R
  • What the org REALLY needs — What the organisation actually needs from this. e.g. Reduce rework in downstream reporting; improve trust
  • What I need to grow next — The capability this builds toward your next step. e.g. Deepen Data Quality mastery; publish an executive DQ guide
  • Still relevant? — Is this still worth your time? e.g. Yes
  • Strategic response — If not fully relevant: renegotiate, redesign, or relocate it. e.g. Not Applicable
  • Evidence / Proof — Proof this is delivering value. e.g. DQ scorecard baseline + monthly trend

Aim for: Honest about relevance — every row carries proof.

Value Question

For each initiative, decide whether your involvement actually changes the outcome.

What to enter

  • Initiative — The initiative you're weighing in on. e.g. Data Quality scorecard + remediation backlog
  • Committed — Are you currently committed to it? (Y/N) e.g. Y
  • Who Benefits — Who gains if this succeeds. e.g. Executive committee; Finance; Analytics teams
  • How Do They Benefit — The benefit they actually feel. e.g. Fewer decision disputes; reduced rework; faster reporting
  • What Changes because I'm Involved — What changes specifically because YOU are involved. e.g. Critical data elements measured monthly and tracked to owners
  • Go / No-Go — Your call: Go, Hold, or No-Go. e.g. Go
  • Metric / Delta — The measure that proves the change. e.g. Rework hours ↓; data defects ↓
  • Next step to clarify — Next step to confirm the value. e.g. Agree top 10 CDEs and DQ thresholds with owners

Aim for: A brutally honest Go / Hold / No-Go with a measurable delta.

Work Analysis

Classify Your Work

Tag where your month goes — no judgement, only clarity.

What to enter

  • Category — Value-Adding, Business-Non-Value-Adding, or Non-Value-Adding. e.g. VA
  • Activity — The activity you're classifying. e.g. Publish DMBOK-aligned Data Quality playbook
  • % of month — Roughly what % of your month this takes. e.g. 15
  • Action strategy — What you'll do about it: add more, automate, reduce, eliminate. e.g. Add more
  • Notes — Why it falls in this category. e.g. Reusable guidance; raises organisational capability

Aim for: Percentages across all rows roughly sum to 100%.

NotePercentages across your rows should roughly add up to 100% of your month — this is what drives your Month Completion Level on the dashboard.

Work Audit most important

Log weekly hours by activity. VA hours tied to a theme count as 'aligned'.

What to enter

  • Date (Week) — The week or date. e.g. Week 1
  • Role — The hat this work was done under — pick from your roles list (a dropdown). The Console uses it to attribute these hours to a department. e.g. Advisory
  • Initiative — Pick the strategic initiative this work serves — the theme fills in automatically. e.g. Deploy data quality monitoring & cleansing processes
  • Strategic Theme — Fills in from the initiative you pick. e.g. Data Quality & Master Data
  • Activity Description — What you actually did. e.g. Cleaned customer data for CRM
  • Category — VA / BNVA / NVA. e.g. VA
  • Time Spent (hrs) — Hours spent. e.g. 3
  • N/A Reason — If BNVA/NVA, why it was needed. e.g. Operational overhead
  • Action Strategy — What to do about it. e.g. Expand – improves accuracy

Aim for: Each row links to an initiative so hours roll up to objectives.

NoteThis is what the Console rolls up. Each row’s Role decides which department the hours count toward, and VA hours tied to an initiative become Business Value-Add. Set the available roles — or load the list your Growth Architect sends — in the ◈ Roles bar at the top of this screen (Import roles list).

Project Scoring

Score initiatives on impact, urgency, growth and effort. Value and ROI compute for you.

What to enter

Exporting & sending

  1. Fill in the sections above — at minimum, the Work Audit with Role, Initiative and Category on every row.

Two views of the same work

WorkbookWhat you do

  • Pick the Initiative your activity supports — that's what makes it Business Value-Add.
  • Set the Role for the row so the right department gets credit.
  • Classify it VA / BNVA / NVA and log the hours.

You're answering: "What business goal did this serve, and under which of my hats?"

ConsoleWhat the Growth Architect sees

  • Your work split across departments — open your card to see the Work split across roles table.
  • Each department's VA-aligned %, hours and work-mix from its slice only.
  • Which strategic initiatives the department is actually covering.

They're answering: "Is my department's effort pointed at what the business asked for?"

How a role is matched to a department

When the Console reads your Role values, it resolves each to a department in this order:

  1. A manual mapping the Growth Architect has set for you (always wins).
  2. A department name match — role Advisory → the Advisory department.
  3. A per-department title match — role LMS Integration → the department where that's your title.
  4. A position name when it's unambiguous — Growth Architect maps to the one department you lead; Executive / Team member likewise when there's only one.

Anything that can't be resolved is shown as Unallocated (in red) and the Growth Architect maps it once, in the Work split across roles editor on your card. Unallocated hours don't count toward any department until mapped.

For the person filling the WorkbookThe cleanest results come from using a consistent Role label that matches how you're set up in the team — usually the department name or your title there. If in doubt, use the department name (Advisory, Training) rather than a free-text job title.
What splits and what doesn't (today)Hours, VA-aligned %, work-mix and initiative coverage split cleanly by role. "Value delivered" and "Quick wins" (from the Value Tracking Log and Project Scoring) are reported per person for now, because those sheets don't yet carry a Role column.

FAQ & troubleshooting

My value statement shows the prompt label instead of my text.

Re-open the latest Console; it reads the real statement from your workbook even on existing snapshots. If you still see the prompt, hard-refresh the page to clear a cached older file.

I ticked a department but the person doesn't appear there.

Individual names appear on the Org Chart and in the Organisation scorecard rows. The change shows the moment you close the panel. If a department sits under an executive whose branch is collapsed, use Expand all.

A VA work item isn't showing under the right department.

Check the Role on that Work Audit row. If it's a position like Growth Architect and the person leads exactly one department, it maps automatically; otherwise open their card and map the role in Work split across roles.

Why is VA-aligned different from my VA percentage?

VA-aligned counts hours that are both VA and on a strategic initiative. VA work that isn't tied to an initiative raises your VA mix but not VA-aligned — the gap is value the business hasn't explicitly asked for.

A department's overview looks like it's counting another role's work.

The Department Overview is scoped to your set department and to the work tagged to that department's role. Make sure each person's Work Audit rows carry the correct Role, and that any unallocated roles are mapped.
