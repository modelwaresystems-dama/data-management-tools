Indicative model for budgeting discussion, not a financial forecast. Drain = capability loss (Non-Value-Add capacity: standardization gap × team cost) + churn cost (turnover × recruitment + ramp). The case is a two-engine programme: Engine 1 certifies a core group (the catalyst that retains them); Engine 2 operationalises & validates the standards across the whole function (the mechanism that recovers capability), so capability recovery scales with the full team, not only those certified. Recovery begins after an editable implementation runway, so payback is shown from operational go-live and the 3-year NPV is ramp-adjusted (year one credited only for its post-go-live months). ELV horizon is modelled as 1 ÷ turnover rate. All assumptions above are editable; figures move with them.
